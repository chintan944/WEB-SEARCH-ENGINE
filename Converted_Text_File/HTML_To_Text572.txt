Arthritis is the joint inflammation and bursitis is the inflammation of a bursa, a fluid-filled sac-like cavity which surrounds the joints. Let us see how arthritis differs from bursitis!Arthritis:Arthritis is an umbrella term that refers to the diseases that cause inflammation in one or more joints. There are more than 150 different types of arthritis out of which Rheumatoid Arthritis and Osteoarthritis are the most common. In the advanced stages of arthritis, one may experience chronic pain and may not be able to walk or climb stairs.Common Symptoms of Arthritis:PainRednessStiffnessSwellingRestricted joint movementCommon arthritis treatment options:MedicinesNon-pharmacological therapyWeight lossSurgeryBursitis:Bursitis is the inflammation of the bursa. Bursa is a soft tissue, a fluid-filled sac-like cavity mostly found over bony prominences like the tip of the elbow, around the hips and knees. It acts as a cushion between a bone and muscle; skin and tendon. This condition generally affects the shoulder, elbow, hips, knees and calf. A person suffering from bursitis often experiences pain, inflammation and tenderness in areas around a joint such as tendons, ligaments, muscles and bursae.Common Causes of Bursitis:Sports injuries or repetitive movementsBad posture or walking habitsStress on soft tissues due to abnormal or poorly positioned jointsMetabolic conditions like diabetesSide effects of certain medicationsCommon bursitis treatment options:Splints, braces or slingsHot and cold therapyOver-the-Counter or Prescription MedicinesCorticosteroids InjectionsPhysical therapyOccupational therapyBased on the above information,
